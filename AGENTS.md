## Skill Emblem Design Standard

- Treat the full skill object as the `skill emblem`: outer shape, material, rim, sheen, shadow, and inner mark.
- Treat the symbol inside the emblem as the `inner mark`.
- Keep skill emblems in a quiet emblem system: balanced, tactile, symbolic, and differentiated through restrained shape, internal marks, or color.
- Borrow from Duolingo's clarity, not its mascot energy: simple rounded shapes, readable silhouettes, limited palettes, and light dimensionality.
- Preserve Ember's warmth: literary, paper-like, brass, wax, ink, ember, vellum, soft leather, and bookish materials.
- Use one dominant outer shape, one central symbol, and no more than one or two supporting accents.
- Limit each emblem to a base, symbol, accent, and one shadow or highlight color when needed.
- Avoid candy-bright palettes, exaggerated cartoon expressions, mascot-like motion, complex illustration, or decorative detail that competes with the skill signal.
- Hover or active motion should be quiet: a small lift, soft shadow, subtle gleam, page-flip shimmer, or tiny nudge.
- Before changing skill emblems, assess whether the change maintains: readable silhouette at small sizes, shared material language, clear hierarchy, and Ember's calm literary tone.
